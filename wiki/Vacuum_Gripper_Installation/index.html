Description

This page describes installation of the Vacuum (Suction) Grippers.

Required Hardware

  • 3mm Allen (Hex) Wrench

Vacuum Gripper Type

Vacuum Gripper v2


Vacuum Gripper v1 (no longer available)


Installation File

PDF: Baxter Vacuum Gripper Guide v2

Step 1: Mount Gripper

This step describes proper attachment of the suction gripper.


A: To install vacuum generator, remove plug with 8mm hex key and in the same port, screw in vacuum generator, red tip first. Hand tighten.

B: Hook Vacuum Cup Gripper onto two posts on tool plate

C: Insert plug into receptacle until you hear a “click”

D: Tighten both screws with enclosed 3mm hex key

Step 2: Install Vacuum Generator

1: Remove Lower Front Panel using 2 thumb screws

2: Squeeze cap collar and pull tubing to remove end caps from supply tube harness. Remove zip tie around tubes.

3: Insert supply tubes into the “P” ports of each valve

4: Using wire bundle attached to plate, connect wires to valves as shown. Valves should be oriented "A” (on front of valve) down, larger valve to outside. Right side is shown

5: Using 2.5 mm hex key, screw valves in place using four M3 x 25mm screws provided

6: Place gray foam gasket on valve as shown

7: On same side as valve install, remove black plastic hole plug from Lower Front Panel

8: After valves are installed, gently re-attach Lower Front Panel. Re-install 2 thumb screws to hold panel in place

Determine air supply tubing diameter. Connect tubing to rear of robot at connector panel.

  • For 1/4” tube, insert tubing into adapter.

9: For 6mm tube, remove adapter then insert tubing into fitting

Step 3: Attach Vacuum Tubing to Arm


Note: Rethink Robotics recommends grounding the Gripper to the Pedestal using the provided ESD ground wire, routing it with tubing.

A: Attach ground wire to same side as Gripper

Remove pedestal bolt on selected side. Attach large ring terminal end of wire and toothed washer to pedestal. Hand- tighten bolt. Using a 17 mm wrench, TIGHTEN A HALF TURN MORE.

B: Attach other end of ground wire as close as possible to vacuum cup

Use part of your vacuum cup configuration, or a manifold block screw boss as shown. Fasten using M4 screw and toothed washer provided.


Note: Make sure valves are installed before adding tubing. Check to ensure tubing is on same arm as installed valves.

1: Starting at the Gripper, insert tubing into Gripper fitting. Push tubing into fitting firmly.

2: Attach tubing (and optional wire) to each arm clip with zip ties, every 50cm as indicated.

3: Firmly tighten zip ties to fix tubing in place. Do not crush tubing. Trim zip ties.

4: Insert tubing end into valve port on Lower Front Panel.

Connect air supply to robot at the connect panel (Baxter's back)

Important: Air supply should be 60 PSI (min) to 90 PSI (max). Air must be filtered and oil-free.


Step 5: Attach Extensions (Optional)

Note: Up to two extensions may be attached to each Vacuum Cup Gripper.

1: Screw in extensions until snug

A: No Extension

B: One Extension

C: Two Extensions

Step 6: Attach Vacuum Cups


A: Select Vacuum Cup

Vacuum Cups should fit entirely on the object surface. Choose the smallest vacuum cup wherever possible. Choose a larger vacuum cup if robot cannot lift the weight of the object.

Note: Baxter can lift a maximum weight of 5 lbs (2.27kg), including weight of gripper which is 0.3 lbs (145 g). Texture, irregular surfaces, items packaged in bags, etc. may require a different vacuum cup selection.

B: Screw on Vacuum Cup until snug

Installation File

PDF: Baxter Vacuum Gripper Guide v1

Step 1: Mount Gripper

This step describes proper attachment of the suction gripper.

A: Hook Suction Gripper onto two posts on hand

B: Insert plug into receptacle until you hear a “click”

C: Fasten Gripper with 3mm Hex and Screws

Step 2: Install Vacuum Generator

Using a 2.5mm hey key, loosen the two screws from the lower front panel.

Remove lower front panel, gently pulling straight out.

1: Plug wiring harness into socket

2: Connect air in

Remove plug from T-connection tubing.


Important: Plug must remain in place if no vacuum generator is attached.


Connect tubing to bottom part of vacuum generator (Air In).

3: Connect air out

Connect tubing exposed coming from the top of the panel to the top port of the Vacuum Generator

4: Attach appropriate control wires

Right Vacuum Gripper - plug wires marked SR-R and BR-R into vacuum generator.

Left Vacuum Gripper - plug wires marked SR-L and BR-L into vacuum generator.

5: Fasten generator to base

Using included screws, fasten generator to metal plate on the same side as the corresponding gripper.

6: Gently re-secure front panel

Step 3: Attach Vacuum Tubing to Arm

Starting at Gripper [1], loosely thread the tubing through the arm clips.


Insert tubing end [6] into the UPPER air inlet. Leave any extra length of tubing between connections [5] and [6].


Push tubing into air inlet until it stops.


Rotate hose so as to not block the hand cameras.


Step 4: Connect air supply to robot

Connect air supply to robot at the connect panel (Baxter's back)

Important: Air supply should be 60 PSI (min) to 90 PSI (max). Air must be filtered and oil-free.


Step 5: Attach Extensions (Optional)

Note: Up to two extensions may be attached to each Vacuum Cup Gripper

Screw in extensions until snug.

A: No Extension

B: One Extension

C: Two Extensions

Step 6: Attach Vacuum Cups

A: Select vacuum cup according to manipulated object.

Vacuum Cups should fit entirely on the object surface. Choose the smallest vacuum cup wherever possible. Choose a larger vacuum cup if the robot cannot lift the weight of the object with a smaller one. Textured or irregular surfaces may require a larger vacuum cup.

Note: Robot can lift a maximum weight of 5 lbs (2.27kg)


B: Screw on selected vacuum cup until snug.
